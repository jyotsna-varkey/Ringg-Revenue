Revenue Strategy · Ringg AI

On GTM
and pricing.

Went deep on Ringg's GTM and pricing. Two things stood out:

01 Channel strategy. Relationship-led GTM is the right foundation. But there's a parallel motion worth building — partnerships with platforms that already have the enterprise relationships Ringg is working to reach. Below is a breakdown of who those partners are and what the ask looks like for each.
02 Outcome-based pricing. Per-minute pricing works for SMBs. For enterprise BFSI, it creates procurement friction. Shifting to outcome-based pricing for enterprise customers — a broader trend in B2B SaaS — frames Ringg as an outcome-oriented service rather than a software tool, and removes the blocker that stalls deals in finance.
01 — Channel Strategy

Building reach
through the right partners

01 Faster than hiring. One channel partnership can put Ringg in front of more customers faster than any individual sales hire. Credgenics alone has 150+ lenders. Getting into their platform is a different order of magnitude from closing one account at a time.
02 Procurement friction disappears. When Ringg comes embedded in a platform the buyer already uses, most of the evaluation work is done. The vendor relationship is established, the integration is live, the security review is inherited. The buyer's decision becomes much simpler.
03 The window is open now. The platforms below are all actively evaluating how to add AI voice to their stack. Some will build it, some will acquire, some will partner. Getting in before they've committed elsewhere is the moment of leverage — that window is roughly 12–18 months wide.
The asymmetry
Credgenics manages 98M+ loan accounts across 150+ lenders. LeadSquared powers CRM for hundreds of BFSI companies. Firstsource runs collections operations for major banks. Each of them has the customer base Ringg wants to reach. None of them has a sub-330ms multilingual voice AI layer. That gap is the basis for genuine partnership — Ringg provides the capability, partners provide the reach.

02 — Pricing Strategy

Selling outcomes,
not minutes

01 Per-minute stalls enterprise deals. When the Collections Head takes a Ringg proposal to their CFO, the first question is: "what will this cost us?" Per-minute pricing requires modelling unknown call volumes, average durations, and connected rates. Most finance teams can't answer that cleanly. The deal stalls while estimates get debated.
02 B2B SaaS is moving toward outcomes anyway. The broader trend is clear — buyers want to pay for results, not usage. For BFSI collections specifically, every rupee spent has a direct equivalent in recovered debt. Outcome-based pricing maps perfectly to how these teams already think about ROI.
03 It reframes what Ringg is. Per-minute pricing positions Ringg as a tool. Per-outcome pricing positions it as a service that delivers results. That's a fundamentally different conversation — and a stronger one for Series B as well.
The shift
Keep per-minute for SMBs and early-stage customers where it works. Introduce outcome-based pricing as an enterprise tier for accounts above a volume threshold — say, 10,000 accounts per month. Customers start on per-minute and graduate to outcome-based as volumes and trust build.
Keep as-is

Per-RPC confirmed

Introduce now

Charge per unique borrower where a right-party contact is confirmed — the AI reached the actual borrower, not a voicemail or wrong number. If the AI can't reach someone, the client doesn't pay.

This maps directly to the first metric a Collections Head cares about, and gives the CFO a clean number: cost per borrower reached.

Per-PTP captured

Charge only when the conversation produces a commitment to pay. The ROI case becomes fully undeniable — clients calculate cost per recovered rupee with a simple formula.

Requires robust PTP tracking in the analytics layer. Worth piloting with one friendly enterprise account to test unit economics before rolling out.

One secondary benefit: outcome-based pricing changes the investor narrative. Per-minute pricing positions Ringg as a usage-based tool. Per-RPC or per-PTP positions it as an outcome-aligned platform — a structurally stronger framing for Series B.
